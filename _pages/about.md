I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Utah. My research sits at the intersection of neuroscience, machine learning, and decision science, with a focus on how neural activity supports risky choice, cognitive control, and reinforcement learning.

I work with intracranial recordings including LFP, sEEG, and single-neuron data, and I build computational models that connect neural measurements to behavior. Alongside human neuroscience research, I have worked on visual cortex recordings, deep learning for signal analysis, and online behavioral task development.

## Research Interests

- Reinforcement learning and decision-making under risk
- Neural mechanisms of cognitive control
- Intracranial electrophysiology and neural data analysis
- Computational neuroscience
- Deep learning for neuroscience applications

## Current Work

- Studying how reinforcement learning models explain behavioral and neural signatures of risky decision-making
- Investigating how interictal epileptiform discharges influence cognitive control and value-based decisions
- Analyzing human intracranial neural recordings from patients undergoing neurosurgical treatment for refractory epilepsy
- Building online behavioral tasks for experimental neuroscience studies

## Background
