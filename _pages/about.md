I am a PhD candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Utah, working in the [Neurosmiths Lab](https://www.neurosmiths.org/). My research sits at the intersection of neuroscience and reinforcement learning, with a focus on how neural activity supports decision-making under risk and cognitive control. I work with intracranial recordings, including LFP and single-neuron data, and I build computational models that connect neural measurements to behavior. I have also developed online behavioral tasks, which can be found on the [Neurosmiths Lab website](https://www.neurosmiths.org/tasks.html).
